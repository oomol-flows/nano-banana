# Nano Banana - AI-Powered Image Editing Made Simple

Transform your images with the power of AI using simple, natural language instructions. Nano Banana makes professional-quality image editing accessible to everyone - no design skills or complex software required.

## What Can You Do?

With Nano Banana, you can describe the changes you want in plain English (or Chinese), and watch as AI transforms your images:

- **Add or remove objects** - "Add a rainbow in the sky" or "Remove the person in the background"
- **Change colors and lighting** - "Make it look like sunset" or "Add dramatic lighting"
- **Transform scenes** - "Turn this into a winter scene" or "Make it look like a painting"
- **Modify details** - "Add flowers to the garden" or "Change the car color to red"
- **Creative edits** - "Add a Pikachu character" or "Make it look vintage"

Simply describe what you want, and the AI understands and applies your changes naturally.

## How It Works

Nano Banana provides you with building blocks that work together seamlessly:

### Starting an Edit

The **Edit Request** block is where your journey begins. You provide:
- The images you want to edit (using web links)
- A description of the changes you want in everyday language

The AI processes your request and gives you a tracking number to check on your results.

### Getting Your Results

The **Get Results** block retrieves your edited images once they're ready. It automatically checks if your images are complete and delivers them to you. You can adjust:
- How often it checks for updates (useful for large batches)
- How long to wait before timing out

### Complete Workflow

The **URL Images Editor** combines both steps into one smooth process. You simply provide your images and instructions, and it handles everything - submitting your request, waiting for processing, and delivering your finished images. This is perfect when you want a hands-off experience.

## Using the Blocks Together

These building blocks are designed to work in harmony. Here are some ways you might combine them:

**For Simple Edits:**
Use the complete workflow block on its own - it's the quickest way to edit images from web links.

**For Batch Processing:**
You might connect multiple edit requests together to process different sets of images with different instructions, all at once.

**For Cloud Storage:**
You can upload local images to cloud storage first, then use those links with the editor - perfect when you're working with files on your computer.

**For Advanced Control:**
Use the individual edit and result blocks separately when you need precise control over timing or want to handle multiple requests simultaneously.
